\paragraph{Eval set size robustness (Section~\ref{sec:eval_calibration}).}
Re-running the constant-$\alpha$ ablation on a $32{,}768$-token WikiText-2
test chunk (chunked into eight $4{,}096$-token windows for memory; baseline
perplexity averaged across windows) yields:
%
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
condition & 4K (Table~1) & 32K (chunked) \\
\midrule
baseline                       & $16.84$       & $11.78$ \\
$\alpha=0.5$ (uniform)         & $2.7\times10^{5}$ & $2.5\times10^{5}$ \\
$\alpha=\bar\alpha$ (per-ch mean) & $6.4\times10^{6}$ & $3.3\times10^{6}$ \\
$\alpha=1$ (bilinear)          & $7.4\times10^{5}$ & $6.1\times10^{5}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
%
The quantitative numbers shift somewhat (the larger eval set is a slightly
easier text distribution), but the qualitative claim is unchanged: every
constant-$\alpha$ replacement destroys the model by $4$--$6$ orders of
magnitude in perplexity at both eval-set sizes.
